Frequently asked questions

~What are the benefits of eating gluten-free foods?

The benefits of a gluten-free diet are largely unsubstantiated. Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. Some people have a condition called celiac disease, which is caused by an intolerance to gluten. Symptoms of celiac disease include diarrhea, weight loss, and fatigue. A gluten-free diet is the only treatment for celiac disease. However, many people without celiac disease choose to follow a gluten-free diet because they believe it is healthier. There is no evidence that a gluten-free diet has any health benefits over a regular diet.

~Can I make substitutions in recipes?

You can make substitutions in recipes, but you need to be careful. If you are substituting ingredients that are not similar in size or volume, you may need to adjust the other ingredients in the recipe as well. Also, be aware that some substitutions may not work well together and may not result in a dish that is as tasty as the original.

~What are the benefits of eating organic foods?

Organic foods are those that are grown without the use of pesticides, synthetic fertilizers, or genetically modified organisms (GMOs). There is no scientific evidence that organic foods are healthier than conventionally grown foods. However, some people prefer to eat organic foods because they believe they are less likely to be contaminated with harmful chemicals.

~Should I use butter or oil when cooking?

It is generally recommended to use oil when cooking, as it has a higher smoke point than butter and is less likely to burn. However, some people prefer the flavor of butter in certain dishes.
